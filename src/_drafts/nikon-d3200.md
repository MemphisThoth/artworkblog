# Nikon D3200

I own a Nikon D3200 digital Single reflex lens camera. It was first released in 2012.

I bought my camera used from [mpb](https://www.mpb.com). I was looking for a company that would provide a warranty rather than buying something from eBay. It cost a bit more, but it came with a six month warranty.

I liked how mpb grades their equipment. For cameras, they show a *shutter count*. My camera had a shutter count of about 1,900. So, it is practically brand new. Most cameras top out at 100,000 shutter count. 

Shipping from mpb was quicker than I expected as well. It arrived in three days rather than the one week that was expected.

The Nikon D3200 is a great camera for beginners. That's what Nikon meant for when it was released.

I had a budget of $300, and I was looking at several cameras from Nikon and Canon that fit the budget. 

Canon had a model called the Canon EOS Rebel t3i. It was released in 2011. The camera had an 18 megapixel sensor, and an articulating screen. I almost bought it for that reason, but I preferred the Nikon brand.

The Nikon D3200 is a 24 megapixel camera with an APS-C sensor. I was surprised at how small and light it is.

I paired it with a Nikon AF-S DX Nikkor 18-55mm f/3.5-5.6G VR lens. This is a zoom lens with autofocus and vibration reduction. 

Altogether, it cost me $195 for the body, and $70 for the lens.

The camera is nice to work with. I use it in full automatic mode for now. I am most concerned about getting good composition in my photos. Later, I intend to learn how to use manual mode.

The 32 Gigabyte SD card holds a thousand RAW images.

It came with a battery and charger too. Later, I purchased the SD card, an SD card reader, and a camera bag. Those I got from Walmart. They were rather inexpensive.

In the future, I want to purchase a 35mm prime lens. That is a lens with a fixed focal point. With the size of the sensor, it works out to a focal length of 52mm. This is close to what the human eye sees. A 55-200mm zoom lens is on my wish list too. Both are rather affordable on the used market.

My wife asked me why I don't just use my phone camera for all my photography. I answered that my phone isn't shaped like a digital SLR camera.
